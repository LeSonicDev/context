### Part III: 核心习语与搭配 (Key Idioms & Collocations)

|**习语/搭配**|**中文释义**|**例句 (Example Sentence)**|
|---|---|---|
|`a nut to crack`|一个难题；一个难懂的人|This maths problem is a tough **nut to crack**.|
|`at least`|至少|It will take **at least** an hour to get there.|
|`bark up the wrong tree`|找错对象；徒劳无功|If you think I'm the one who broke the vase, you're **barking up the wrong tree**.|
|`fish out of water`|格格-入（的环境或人）|As a non-scientist, I felt like a **fish out of water** at the conference.|
|`food for thought`|引人深思的事物|The documentary provided a lot of **food for thought**.|
|`head over heels`|神魂颠倒；完全地|He fell **head over heels** in love with her.|
|`in the wake of`|紧随...之后；作为...的结果|Security was tightened **in the wake of** the recent attacks.|
|`on the same page`|达成共识，意见一致|Before we start, let's make sure we are all **on the same page**.|
|`regardless of`|不管，不顾|The law applies to everyone, **regardless of** their social status.|
|`take... for granted`|认为...理所当然|We should not **take** our clean water **for granted**.|
|`think outside the box`|跳出固有思维模式|The designer is famous for **thinking outside the box**.|
|`tooth-and-nail`|竭尽全力地（尤指斗争）|They fought **tooth-and-nail** to save their company from bankruptcy.|
|`turn the other cheek`|逆来顺受|When his rival insulted him, he chose to **turn the other cheek**.|
|`upset the apple cart`|打乱计划；破坏局面|We had the party all planned, but the sudden rain **upset the apple cart**.|
